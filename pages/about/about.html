About Us

About the Practitioner

Hannah Conrad

CBT Practitioner

My name is Hannah Conrad. When I was in my early 20's, I got to witness the power of CBT in my own life first-hand. In working with my own CBT therapist, I was amazed how a few simple techniques allowed me to emotionally recover from experiences in my own past. Don't get me wrong; it took real work. The results made such an impact that over the next decade, I continuously looked for ways to apply these principles. I looked for ways to help others apply them too.

Then in 2023, my husband and I moved to Bangalore, India. This change afforeded me extra time which I was able to take courses in CBT. I was thrilled to get a chance to get more involved with something I'm so passionate about.

About New Pathways Cognitive Coaching

Over the years, I have seen how many with the desire to improve their emotional health are often limited by the high financial cost of therapy. Well, toward the end of my course in 2023, I began offering "sessions" to some I knew personally. The response was really incredible. Those who had just a few sessions began recommending my services to others. I quickly began to realize that, while some people in the world need the help of a licensed therapist, others simply need a little guidance in identifying the core beliefs and thoughts that are robbing them of peace. That's when the idea of New Pathways Cognitive Coaching first entered my head.

How wonderful it would be to have a platform for qualified CBT practitioners to offer guidance to those needing support at an affordable cost. I began working on a path that potential clients could follow to help them overcome common problems. That path helps them to create new patterns of thinking - new pathways of thought. Then, in December 2023, New Pathways Cognitive Coaching was ready to begin.

The pathway I created takes you through about 6 months of CBT techniques.

Weeks 1-4

Establish Values and Goals

Weeks 5-8

Developing Healthy Habits

Weeks 9-12

Overcoming Thought Distortions

Weeks 13-16

Identifying Core Beliefs

Weeks 17-24

Expanding and Maintaining Our Emotional Tool Box

Frequently Asked Questions

What are "core beliefs"?

A core belief is a belief, conscious or not, that shapes how an individual views themself and how interactions with others are perceived. For example, the core belief "I am inadequate," may lead someone to feel hesitant to try new things. It may cause a feeling of shame or anxiety after engaging in an activity, even if such an activity was successful. This core belief may cause them perceive the passing comments and actions of others as being veiled criticisms. It may even produce additional social anxiety.

What are "thought distortions"?

Thought distortions include common reasoning that feels valid but lacks evidence. A few examples include:

  • Catastrophizing
  • Filtering
  • Jumping to Conclusions
  • Discounting the Positive
  • Personalizing

What are SMART goals?

SMART is an acronym for goals that are:

  • Specific - The desired outcome of the goal is clearly identified.
  • Measurable - The goal produces data that can be reviewed and tracked.
  • Achievable - The final goal must be realistic.
  • Relevant - The outcome should clearly align with what you want your life and health to look like in the future.
  • Time-bound - The goal must have a deadline.

Is a CBT practitioner a therapist?

No. CBT practitioners are not licensed therapists. However, they do have a strong grasp of CBT techniques which allow them to provide practical assistance to those seeking help.

What habits will New Pathways help me to form?

New Pathways will help you identify ways to create better habits for sleeping, eating, exercising, breathing, dealing with conflict, and more.

How long should I continue seeing a CBT practitioner?

Every person is different with different needs. However, we recommend somewhere between 8 - 24 weekly sessions. Individuals dedicated to improving their emotional health can put CBT methods into practice after only 1-2 sessions. Many find it helpful to extend sessions in order to form concrete habits with the help of a CBT practitioner.
